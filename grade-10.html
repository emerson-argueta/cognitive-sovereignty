Phase Three · Grade 10

The Last Year to Change Course

Deep specialization begins in Grade 11. Grade 10 is the final wide-angle year.

Track movement in Grade 10 remains as frictionless as in Grade 9 — a conversation, a willingness to try, done. But students feel the clock ticking. By the end of this year, they will have identified their primary track. The Grade 11 transition is real.

All four tracks begin producing things with real stakes this year. No more simulations for their own sake — real mentors, real materials, real audiences, real consequences when something doesn't work.

Academic Track · Grade 10

Research Methodology at Depth

Study design, peer review, the replication crisis — the machinery of knowledge production.

What Students Practice

  • Study design: control groups, double-blinding, confounding variables, statistical power
  • Peer review: what it is, what it catches, what it misses, why it remains the best system we have
  • The replication crisis: why many famous studies failed to replicate and what this means for scientific knowledge
  • Original research project: a genuine question, a genuine methodology, genuine data collection and analysis
  • Formal paper: structured as an academic article — abstract, introduction, methods, results, discussion
Vocational Track · Grade 10

First Real Apprenticeship

Real mentors, real materials, real consequences.

What Students Practice

  • Active apprenticeship placement begins — minimum 2 days per week in a real trade environment
  • Advanced contract and regulatory literacy: reading real employment agreements, safety regulations, union contracts
  • Financial reasoning: job costing, material estimation, quote preparation — real numbers for real work
  • Documenting decisions: every non-trivial choice in an apprenticeship task is recorded and explained
  • Evaluating a real product or service claim using the full Phase Two analytical toolkit
Systems & Engineering Track · Grade 10

Design Under Real Constraint

The first build that has to work — with real materials, real failure, real accountability.

What Students Practice

  • A semester-long design project with defined real-world constraints: budget, materials, performance requirements
  • Failure analysis: when the first design fails (it will), the failure is documented, analyzed, and fed back into the redesign
  • Studying real engineering failures: the Tacoma Narrows Bridge, the Challenger O-rings, the Boeing 737 MAX MCAS — what reasoning errors caused them?
  • Computational thinking deepened: working with real data, real algorithms, real debugging
  • Formal constraint documentation: every design decision recorded against the constraints it satisfies or violates
Civic Track · Grade 10

Policy Design — First Attempt

A real policy proposal. A real critic. A real revision.

What Students Practice

  • Identifying a real local or regional policy problem with genuine stakes
  • Designing a policy proposal: the problem statement, the proposed intervention, the evidence for it, the likely objections
  • Presenting the proposal to a real external critic — someone who has a reason to disagree
  • Revising under pressure: incorporating legitimate criticism into a stronger version of the proposal
  • Legal reasoning introduced: how does law work? What is a precedent? How are statutes interpreted?
What Grade 10 Builds

Ready for Grade 11 Specialization

  • 1
    Has produced something real in their primary trackA research paper, an apprenticeship portfolio, an engineering build, or a revised policy proposal — with real stakes.
  • 2
    Has experienced genuine failure and documented itIn every track, something went wrong this year. The response to failure — analysis, documentation, redesign — is the education.
  • 3
    Has identified their primary track with confidenceNot because they were assigned to it, but because they have spent two years discovering it.
  • 4
    Maintains cross-track fluency in all four orientationsThe annual fluency modules ensure no track becomes a silo.
  • 5
    Is ready for deep specialization in Grade 11The calling is identified. The tools are proven. The stakes are real. Grade 11 goes deep.
