Structural Model

The Five Lenses

Each week is anchored by a Core Problem, not a logical theme — explored through five disciplines simultaneously.

Rather than treating subjects as separate containers, 7th grade organizes every week around a single real-world problem. Boolean logic, argument mapping, data analysis, and visualization are not taught in isolation — they are all applied to the same problem from different angles within the same day.

The Vertical Thread

The Metacognitive Layer

Grade 7 is where the Internal Heatmap becomes an active analysis tool, not just a feeling label.

In 6th grade, students learned to notice their physiological responses to charged information. In 7th grade, that observation becomes a formal step in every analytical process — the first gate through which any claim must pass before logical tools are applied.

The Internal Heatmap Protocol

Step 1 — Notice: Before engaging with a claim, students pause and name their physiological response. Does this headline produce immediate agreement, anger, or fear?

Step 2 — Name: The emotional state is recorded in the Thinking Journal — not to suppress it, but to treat it as data. A strong reaction is a signal that a manipulation lever may have been pulled.

Step 3 — Gate: The Logic Gate stays closed until the emotional pulse subsides. Only then are the Toulmin Model and Boolean tools applied.

Step 4 — Map: In the Metacognitive Reflection session, students map both the argument's structure and their own response to it — noting which emotional lever was pulled and why it works.

This is also the year when Red Team Inoculation Exercises are introduced for the first time — under mandatory Certified Facilitator supervision. Students construct (but never publish) a piece of persuasive misinformation, then immediately use their logic tools to dismantle it.

Facilitation note: Red Team exercises in 7th grade are introductory. The Socratic Debrief Protocol is mandatory and non-negotiable. A poorly facilitated debrief does not produce a neutral outcome — it produces cynicism. These sessions require a Certified Facilitator.

Branching Competency

Three Tracks, One Standard

Movement between tracks requires nothing more than a conversation. No tests. No stigma.

All three tracks use the same Five Lenses, the same Core Problems, and the same assessment standards. The difference is the context in which the tools are applied — not the rigor with which they are learned.

Core Competency

Required · All Students
  • Toulmin Model: Claim → Grounds → Warrant → Rebuttal
  • Boolean logic: AND / OR / NOT in natural language
  • Recognizing Appeal to Authority, Cherry-Picking, Slippery Slope, False Dichotomy
  • Source credibility evaluation with the CRAAP Test
  • Introductory Red Team exercise with full debrief

Extension Competency

For Students Who Have Mastered Core
  • Full formal Toulmin Flowcharts with rebuttal branches
  • P-value and confidence interval interpretation
  • Boolean circuit diagrams and logic gate maps
  • Steel-manning opposing arguments in written form
  • Preparation for Grade 8 predicate logic

Vocational Depth

High-Intensity Application Track
  • Boolean If/Then rules applied to trade or agricultural decision-making
  • Reading a real contract using Toulmin argument mapping
  • Spotting manipulated graphs in product or supplier claims
  • Evaluating material safety data sheets as statistical arguments
  • Designing a simple decision tree for a real-world task

The Lateral Move: If a student stalls on Boolean circuit diagrams or formal Toulmin structure, the Orchestrator shifts them into a High-Intensity Application week. They apply the tools they already have to a real legal brief or product specification — without repeating abstract exercises.

Mastery-Based Assessment

How Mastery Is Measured

No standardized tests. No multiple choice. Evidence of thinking only.

Assessment in 7th grade measures the quality of reasoning, not the speed of recall. Every tool is designed to capture how a student thinks, not just whether they arrived at a correct answer.

Project Rubric

Weekly · 5-point scale

Evaluates logical rigor, visual clarity, and claim construction in the week's project output.

Logic Chain Accuracy

Quarterly review

Every claim traces to evidence. Every warrant is explicit. Gaps in the chain are the primary feedback signal.

Fallacy Log

1 per quarter

Students document real instances of their assigned fallacy found in media, conversation, or their own writing.

Portfolio

Quarterly

Student-curated evidence of their best thinking. Includes work from the Development Key selected by the student.

Self-Reflection

Weekly Thinking Journal

"What bias did I catch this week?" The metacognitive record is private to student and teacher.

Capstone Defense

End of each quarter

A formal live presentation defending the quarter's project before a panel. Logic only — no fallacies.

Two-Keys Portfolio: All process data — drafts, failed logic chains, Fallacy Logs, Thinking Journal entries — is stored in the private Development Key and purged at year end unless the student chooses to retain it. The Credential Key issues only verified competency certificates, with no timestamp or process data visible externally.

End-of-Year Outcomes

What a Grade 7 Graduate Can Do

Preparation for Grade 8 Synthesis — and for life in an information environment designed to manipulate.

  • 1
    Build a visual argument map using the full Toulmin structure Claim, Grounds, Warrant, Backing, Qualifier, and Rebuttal — all explicit, all connected, all defensible.
  • 2
    Write a 5-paragraph essay with counterargument and data Nuanced thesis, evidence integration, citation, and concession with rebuttal.
  • 3
    Identify 6 major fallacies in any source Appeal to Authority, Cherry-Picking, Slippery Slope, False Dichotomy, False Equivalence, and Correlation/Causation confusion.
  • 4
    Code a simple Boolean logic gate in Scratch Translating natural-language conditional rules into functional computational logic.
  • 5
    Win a debate using logic, not emotion A live formal debate with cross-examination, defense of warrant, and zero informal fallacies.
  • 6
    Recognize their own physiological response before engaging with a claim The Internal Heatmap is now an automatic first step — not a taught exercise, but a habit of mind.

Ready for Grade 8 → Synthesis: Predicate Logic (∀, ∃), scientific skepticism, statistical fallacies, and the full Capstone Defense. The Toulmin Model mastered in 7th grade becomes the scaffold for everything that follows.
