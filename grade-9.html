Phase Three · Grade 9

Still Finding the Calling

Track intensity increases — but no student locks in before Grade 11.

Phase Three is where the tools get applied with genuine stakes and real autonomy. But Grade 9 is explicitly not the year for premature specialization. The adolescent identity is still forming. Students who are pushed into a permanent track at 14 often discover at 18 that it was the wrong one.

Grade 9 is high-intensity breadth: every student experiences all four track orientations in depth, while beginning to invest more time in the one or two that feel most alive.

Track movement in Grade 9 requires nothing more than a conversation. No tests, no formal re-enrollment, no stigma. The Orchestrator makes lateral moves easy.

The Studio Model

Compelled Labor vs Chosen Labor

The day ends at 1 PM. The building stays open until 5 PM.

8:00 AM – 1:00 PM · Core Instruction

  • All required instruction, seminars, and guided deep work
  • The 35/15 rhythm continues
  • All required work completed here — Zero Homework honored
  • Cross-Track Fluency module included every day

1:00 PM – 5:00 PM · The Resourced Studio

  • Facilities remain open as mentored project spaces
  • Staff present — available but not directing
  • Attendance entirely voluntary
  • Technical assistance and safety oversight provided
  • A student who stays until 5 PM because they can't put down what they're building is showing you their calling.
Track Orientations

Four Directions, One Year

All four tracks in Grade 9 — breadth before depth.

Academic

Construction of knowledge
  • Philosophy of science: how knowledge is built, tested, overturned
  • Formal logic and proof theory at introductory depth
  • Rhetoric and the history of argument
  • Research methodology: study design, peer review, replication

Vocational

Skilled trades · agriculture · craft
  • Applied logic in real trade contexts: contracts, specs, safety docs
  • Financial literacy: predatory terms, loans, pricing structures
  • Introduction to apprenticeship with real mentors
  • Systems thinking: how a building, farm, or machine works as a whole

Systems & Engineering

Doers who are also thinkers
  • Systems modeling: how complex systems fail and why
  • Design under constraint: iterative building, prototyping, failure analysis
  • Computational thinking: algorithms, data structures, logic of machines
  • Hands-on engineering introduction projects

Civic & Ethical

Thread through all tracks
  • Policy reasoning: evaluating proposals with logic tools, not tribal affiliation
  • Institutional analysis: how governments, courts, and media distort incentives
  • Moral philosophy as rigorous questions, not answers
  • Democratic participation: a real local civic issue researched and presented
Cross-Track Fluency

No Track Is an Island

Every student in every track maintains fluency across all four — every semester.

Cross-track fluency is the structural guardrail against the system hardening into class stratification. The 'thinkers' and the 'doers' model is exactly what this curriculum was designed to prevent.

Civic Track — All Students

Democratic Participation as Practice

Not as a subject. As an act.

Grade 9 Civic Project — All Students

  • Every student, in every track, identifies a real local civic issue
  • Research the issue using all Phase Two tools: source evaluation, statistical analysis, argument mapping
  • Present a formal analysis and position to a real external audience — not a class, a real audience
  • The project is not graded on the position taken — it is graded on the quality of the reasoning
